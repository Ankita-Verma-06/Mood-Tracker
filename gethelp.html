Frequently Asked Questions

What do I do if I’m worried about my mental health?


The most important thing is to talk to someone you trust. This might be a friend, colleague, family member, or GP. In addition to talking to someone, it may be useful to find out more information about what you are experiencing.

How can I help a friend struggling with their mental health?


Listen without judgment, encourage them to talk about their feelings, and suggest professional help if needed. Let them know they’re not alone.

What do I do if the support doesn’t help?


Finding the right help can be challenging since different things work for different people. It's essential to stay open to various approaches, remain persistent in your search, and keep hope alive—even when some solutions don’t work as expected.

How can I manage stress and anxiety effectively?


Managing stress requires self-care techniques such as meditation, exercise, maintaining a balanced diet, and ensuring proper sleep. Talking to someone you trust and setting realistic goals can also help in reducing stress.

Where can I find professional mental health support?


You can seek help from licensed therapists, counselors, or mental health professionals. Online mental health platforms, helplines, and support groups can also be great resources if you’re unsure where to start.
